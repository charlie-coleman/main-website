The 27-card Trick
The 27-card trick is a variation on the 21-card trick. The trick utilizes the Gergonne p-pile principle to place a card at any point in the deck. The Gergonne principle was developed by Joseph Diaz Gergonne when he was alive in the 18th or 19th century. The card trick has gone through many variations over the past couple hundred years, and in this set of instructions, we will just be talking about the 27 card version of the 21 card trick

Required Supplies
  • Cards
  • A Volunteer
Warnings
Be careful when:
  • dealing the cards
  • picking up the piles of cards
Doing either incorrectly will cause issues.

How to: Math
To begin, we must first learn a little math. We are basically just converting a base-10 number into base-3. It's a very simple conversion.

For a visual, click each step to change the image on the right.
  • Decide on a number.
    • We'll use 7 for this example
  • Divide the number by 3. The remainder is the most important part.
    • Dividing 7, we get 2 with a remainder of 1
  • Divide the number you got from step 2 by 3
    • Dividing 2, we get 0 with a remainder of 2
  • Divide that number by 3 once more
    • Dividing 0, we get 0 with a remainder of 0
  • Now put the remainders into one list of numbers, going from right to left.
    • From the remainders we got, we end up with 021 as our final number
Remember this number. It will be important later in the trick.
Setup


1) Take any 27 cards to be used for the trick


2) Offer the volunteer their pick of any card. They should memorize it and place it back into the deck anywhere. Shuffle the cards before continuing.


3) The card used for this demonstration will be the King of Spades

Dealing the Cards



4) Place the cards one at a time in three piles, from left to right. Our card is in the far right pile.



5) After placing a card in the far right pile, return to the far left pile and continue dealing until you are out of cards.

6) After finishing dealing the cards, you have to refer to the base-3 number we are using to decide the order the cards are picked up in. The first deal looks at the first digit on the right. A 0 gets picked up last, 1 gets picked up second, and 2 gets picked up first. The other 2 piles can be picked up in any order. Our number is 021, the first digit is a 1 so we pick it up second.

7) Deal the cards into 3 piles again. Our card is in the far right pile again.

8) The second digit in our base-3 number is 2, meaning we pick up the pile with our card in it first.


9) Deal the cards again. This time our card is in the far left pile.

10) The last digit in our number is a 0, meaning we pick up the pile with our card in it last.
Finishing Move


11) Finally, you can count to your chosen number and place down a card for each number. When you get to your number, the next card will be theirs.
